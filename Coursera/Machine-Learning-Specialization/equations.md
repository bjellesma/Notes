**Univariate Linear Regression** - $\hat{y}^{(i)} = wx^{(i)} + b$

**Squared Error Cost Function** -  $J(w,b) = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{m} (f_{w,b}(x^{(i)}) - y^{(i)})^{2}}{2m}$ where m is the number of training examples, $\hat{y}^(i)$ or $f_{w,b}(x^{(i)}$ is the value of the line that we're proposing, and $y^{(i)}$ is the actual value.
